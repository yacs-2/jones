Essential Literary Critique

The Hobbit

"The Hobbit" by J.R.R. Tolkien is a classic fantasy adventure story, perfect for both children and adults, featuring the home-loving hobbit Bilbo Baggins who unexpectedly embarks on a quest with a group of dwarves to reclaim their treasure from a dragon, discovering courage and self-reliance along the way; praised for its charming narrative, well-developed characters, and immersive world-building, making it a great introduction to Middle-earth.

Animal Farm

"Animal Farm" is a satirical allegorical novella, in the form of a beast fable,by George Orwell, first published in England on 17 August 1945. It tells the story of a group of anthropomorphic farm animals who rebel against their human farmer, hoping to create a society where the animals can be equal, free, and happy. Ultimately, the rebellion is betrayed, and under the dictatorship of a pig named Napoleon, the farm ends up in a state far worse than before.

Narrative of The Life of Fredrick Douglass

The narrative of the life of fredrick douglass is about how he became the most important leader of the movement for African American civil rights in the 19th century. After escaping from slavery in Maryland, Douglass became a national leader of the abolitionist movement in Massachusetts and New York, during which he gained fame for his oratory and incisive antislavery writings.
